The weekly bulletin from City University of Macau Faculty of Finance

China Dynamics

This week, China's financial market experienced moderate volatility. The Shanghai Composite Index saw a slight rise at Monday's opening but dipped slightly on Wednesday due to global economic uncertainties. The Chinese government announced a series of economic stimulus policies, including tax cuts and increased infrastructure investment, which boosted market confidence to some extent. The RMB exchange rate remained stable, and the central bank maintained reasonable liquidity through open market operations.

Global Dynamics

Global financial markets also faced fluctuations this week. U.S. stocks rose after the Federal Reserve announced it would keep interest rates unchanged, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average and S&P 500 both posting gains. European markets, however, were impacted by Brexit uncertainties, leading to a decline in the FTSE 100. In Asia, Japan's Nikkei 225 faced pressure due to a stronger yen. Global crude oil prices rose this week, driven by escalating tensions in the Middle East.

Weekly Performance of the World's Top Five Stock Markets

United States: Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 1.5%, and the S&P 500 gained 1.2%.

China: The Shanghai Composite Index edged up 0.3%, while the Shenzhen Component Index rose 0.5%.

Japan: The Nikkei 225 fell 0.8%.

Germany: The DAX Index dropped 0.5%.

United Kingdom: The FTSE 100 declined 1.0%.

Weekly Economic News Digest

1. Macau: Macau released its latest tourism data this week, showing a 15% year-on-year increase in visitor numbers, primarily driven by a rise in mainland Chinese tourists. The Macau government also announced increased investment in the tourism sector to further enhance its appeal as an international travel destination.

2. Mainland China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan: Mainland China unveiled a series of economic stimulus measures this week, including tax reductions and increased infrastructure spending, aimed at stabilizing economic growth. Hong Kong's stock market remained stable, with the Hang Seng Index rising slightly by 0.2%. In Taiwan, strong performances in the tech sector drove the Taiwan Weighted Index higher.

3. International Economy: This week, the Federal Reserve announced it would maintain interest rates, a decision in line with market expectations. The European Central Bank hinted at further monetary policy easing in the near future to address slowing economic growth. Global crude oil prices rose due to tensions in the Middle East, with Brent crude increasing by 3% this week.
